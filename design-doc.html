Introduction

The goal of our project is to immerse the user in an interactive narrative, the setting will take place in a forest environment. We plan on using soft tones to create a vibrant and positive setting the user will enjoy spending time in. In addition to, we plan on pursuing a low-poly design which will allow for a cartoon design, allowing the player to use their imagination when interacting over photo-realism. The basic narrative will follow the player around as they follow a linear path in pursuit of their lost dog. During the event of looking for the missing dog, players will be required to solve a variety of puzzles, these will be based on appropriate settings, for example if the player comes across a river, then they must use available items in their location to build a boat. Some narration, including cinematic animation- where players are unable to move will be used. The goal is to create an adventure that the players can be immersed in through sound, and visuals. ** may require 2 additional camera to allow players to turn back to the screen and retain the same position in the game and out, without clipping.

Description

The focus of the experience will be on world mood since it is a narrative, therefore audio and visual objects matching the tone of the game will be important. The time of day the game will take place is sunset to sunrise, to bring life to the world. The player will enjoy a beautiful and colourful world as they explore a small area with their companion before a storm will appear out of nowhere and cause the dog to run off. After which, the game will change tones from happy and playful to panic and a darker tone/music will grip the experience as the player will be required to enter into a forest in order to find the dog. hey will be guided through potential light cues and footprints/fur on the ground. After overcoming a set amount of events, the player will learn where the companion has ended up the game will gradually shift back to a uplighting tone as they reunite.

Story milestones and interaction events;

  1. Crossing the river: use a floating bark and a branch as a paddle.
  2. Crossing poison ivy: use boots which are hidden inside the first cave.
  3. Entering the first cave: move the vines.
  4. Opening the door to the second cave: pull down a rope and tie it to a solid clip/hook.
  5. Getting the dog: climb up the side of the cliff.

Key Features

Assets

  1. VRTK for player interaction
  2. Low-poly prefabs for the environment (trees, rocks, plants, poison ivy, vines, stone door,etc.)
  3. Interactive objects outside the environment eg. boots, rope and tree bark/branch.
  4. Sound Components: Static music, soft environment (example) SFX will include forest animals: birds, water moving, wind, etc.
  5. Animation: Grabbing,, walking,paddling, picking, moving, pulling .
